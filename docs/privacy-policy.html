GlyphMaps — Privacy Policy

Effective 14 July 2026 · applies to the GlyphMaps Android app (com.glyphnavtoy)
The short version: GlyphMaps has no servers, no accounts, no analytics, and no ads. It reads one thing — Google Maps' turn-by-turn navigation notifications — entirely on your phone, to draw the current maneuver on your Nothing Phone's Glyph Matrix. Nothing you do ever leaves the device.

What the app accesses

GlyphMaps uses Android's Notification Access (a NotificationListenerService). With your permission, granted through system settings, it reads the content of Google Maps navigation notifications only — the maneuver, distance to the turn, street name, ETA, and route progress. Notifications from every other app are ignored and never inspected.

How that data is used

  • Parsed in memory, on the device, to render the turn arrow and distance on the Glyph Matrix LEDs.
  • Shown inside the app while you navigate (the same information Google Maps is already showing you).
  • Discarded when navigation ends. It is not stored, logged, or written to disk in the release app.

What is collected or shared

Nothing. GlyphMaps collects no personal data, no location data (it has no location permission), no identifiers, and no usage analytics. It makes no network requests: there is no server component, no third-party SDK that transmits data, and no advertising. Data cannot be sold or shared because none is collected.

Data stored on your device

Your preferences (LED brightness, display mode, whether onboarding is complete) are saved in the app's private storage on your phone and are deleted when you uninstall the app.

Children

GlyphMaps is not directed at children and collects no data from anyone.

Changes

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this address with a new effective date. Since the app collects nothing, changes are expected to be rare and editorial.
